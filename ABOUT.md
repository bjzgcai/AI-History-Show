# About AI History Show

## About AI History

AI History Show is an open-source interactive project for exhibitions, classrooms, and public communication.

The project organizes AI history as a knowledge interface rather than a flat list of years. It connects events, people, places, papers, images, sources, and explanations into one navigable experience.

- **AI history timeline**: builds an overall path from the Turing Test and the Dartmouth Workshop to deep learning and the large model era.
- **Top AI achievements**: organizes important algorithms, systems, papers, and applications, with maps, images, sources, and concise interpretation.
- **Thematic storylines**: expands into focused topics such as AI for Science, AI in games, AI and education, and AI, society, and ethics.

The goal is to make AI history easier to inspect, verify, and explain: a historical map, a source archive, and an interactive storytelling system.

GitHub repository: <https://github.com/bjzgcai/AI-History-Show>

## What We Explain

AI history is not just a list of years. It is a network of people, institutions, papers, experiments, tools, and social needs.

The project helps audiences understand when and where events happened, why certain algorithms appeared, what problems they solved, and how later technologies were shaped by them.

- Show events through map, timeline, archive images, source records, and readable explanations.
- Keep sources visible so facts can be checked, cited, and improved.
- Help non-specialist audiences understand AI breakthroughs without requiring a technical background.

## Join Us

We welcome contributors from computer science, AI, design, interaction, history, science communication, frontend development, and translation.

You can start small: check one event, add sources, polish wording, improve layout, contribute images, or create a quiz that helps audiences confirm they understood the story.

Recommended path:

1. Open the GitHub repository and read the README files and existing pages.
2. Choose an event, person, algorithm, or storyline that interests you.
3. Create or claim an Issue, then submit a Pull Request with a clear summary and source information.

## Shared Vision

In the long run, we hope AI History Show can become open infrastructure for AI history exhibitions, courses, and public learning.

AI history is still being written. We want to make it clear, beautiful, traceable, and useful for more classrooms, museums, students, and communities.

- Run reliably on large screens in exhibitions.
- Support AI courses, public lectures, and guided learning.
- Help students learn AI history by contributing content and verifying sources.
- Help the public understand where AI came from and where it may be going.
