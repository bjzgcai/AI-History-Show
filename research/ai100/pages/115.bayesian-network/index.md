## One-Sentence Summary
Bayesian Network is an AI100 achievement in Probabilistic graphical models.

## Hero Description
Bayesian networks gave AI a compact language for uncertain reasoning. A directed graph encodes dependencies among variables, while conditional probability tables quantify how evidence should change beliefs. The idea became central to diagnosis, sensor fusion, decision support, and later causal modeling.

## People & Place
- Judea Pearl: Pioneer of Bayesian networks and causal AI

Key place: University of California, Los Angeles

## Historical Background
Symbolic AI struggled with uncertainty. Probabilistic graphical models connected knowledge representation with statistical inference.

## Canonical Source
Bayesian Networks technical paper. https://ftp.cs.ucla.edu/pub/stat_ser/R246.pdf

## Core Idea
A Bayesian network factorizes a joint distribution according to a directed acyclic graph, making inference modular.

## Key Concepts
- Directed Acyclic Graph: Nodes are variables and arrows express dependency structure.
- Conditional Probability Table: Each node stores probabilities given its parents.
- Belief Updating: Evidence changes probabilities throughout the graph.

## Impact
Bayesian networks shaped probabilistic AI and later causal reasoning frameworks.
