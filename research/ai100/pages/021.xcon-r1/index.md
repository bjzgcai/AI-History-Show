# XCON-R1

## Full Name
XCON-R1

## Year / Period
1978-1980

## Type
Expert System

## One-Sentence Summary
XCON/R1 is an AI100 achievement in expert systems.

## Hero Description
XCON turned expert-system research into a visible industrial success. Built with production rules, it configured DEC VAX computer orders by matching customer requirements, component constraints, and assembly knowledge, reducing costly configuration errors at commercial scale.

## People & Place
- John McDermott: Developer of R1/XCON

Key place: Carnegie Mellon University

## Historical Background
Computer systems were sold as many interacting components, and human configuration errors could delay or break orders.

## Canonical Source
R1: An Expert in the Computer Systems Domain. https://ojs.aaai.org/index.php/AAAI/article/view/8022

## Core Idea
Production rules matched facts in working memory, fired actions, and incrementally built a valid hardware configuration.

## Key Concepts
- Production Rule: If-then rules encode configuration expertise.
- Working Memory: Current order facts are updated as rules fire.
- Knowledge Engineering: Expert knowledge is elicited, encoded, and maintained.

## Impact
XCON became a standard case study for knowledge engineering, expert-system maintenance, and AI's early business value.
