# Frame

## Full Name
A Framework for Representing Knowledge

## Year / Period
1974

## Type
Knowledge Representation

## One-Sentence Summary
Frame is an AI100 achievement in Knowledge representation.

## Hero Description
Frames proposed a way to represent stereotyped situations as structured bundles of slots, defaults, expectations, and procedures. Instead of treating knowledge as isolated facts, frames organize what is normally true and what to check next. They influenced semantic networks, object-oriented knowledge systems, and the way AI systems model context.

## People & Place
- Marvin Minsky: Author of the frame representation proposal

Key place: MIT Artificial Intelligence Laboratory

## Historical Background
Symbolic AI needed richer structures than flat predicate lists. Frames offered reusable templates for scenes and concepts.

## Canonical Source
A Framework for Representing Knowledge. https://courses.media.mit.edu/2004spring/mas966/Minsky%201974%20Framework%20for%20knowledge.pdf

## Core Idea
A frame stores slots such as parts, roles, defaults, and attached procedures; missing information can be filled by expectations.

## Key Concepts
- Slot: A named field in a frame, such as color, owner, or expected part.
- Default Value: A typical assumption used until evidence says otherwise.
- Inheritance: Specific frames can reuse knowledge from more general frames.

## Impact
Frames became a central knowledge-representation idea and foreshadowed object-like AI data structures.
