# IBM Watson

## Full Name
IBM Watson

## Year / Period
2006-2011

## Type
Question Answering

## One-Sentence Summary
IBM Watson is an AI100 achievement in question answering and natural-language AI.

## Hero Description
IBM Watson showed that open-domain question answering could be engineered as a large evidence-ranking pipeline. For Jeopardy!, DeepQA generated candidate answers, gathered many signals, scored supporting evidence, and answered quickly enough to compete with elite human players.

## People & Place
- David Ferrucci: Principal investigator of the Watson / DeepQA team
- IBM DeepQA team: Built the question-answering system

Key place: IBM Thomas J. Watson Research Center

## Historical Background
After Deep Blue, IBM looked for a language-heavy challenge where ambiguity, clues, timing, and confidence estimation mattered.

## Canonical Source
Watson: Beyond Jeopardy!. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.artint.2012.06.009

## Core Idea
Watson did not rely on a single parser or database. It generated hypotheses, searched sources, scored evidence, and merged rankings into a final answer with confidence.

## Key Concepts
- Hypothesis Generation: Candidate answers are generated from many interpretations of the clue.
- Evidence Scoring: Independent scorers measure support from passages, entities, dates, and relations.
- Confidence Ranking: Watson decides whether to answer based on merged confidence.

## Impact
Watson became a landmark for industrial natural-language AI, while also revealing how difficult it is to turn showcase question answering into robust domain products.
