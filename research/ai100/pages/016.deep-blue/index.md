# Deep Blue

## Full Name
Deep Blue journal article

## Year / Period
1997

## Type
Search

## One-Sentence Summary
Deep Blue is an AI100 achievement in Game AI.

## Hero Description
Deep Blue became the first computer system to defeat the reigning world chess champion in a regulation match. The achievement mixed brute-force alpha-beta search, chess-specific evaluation, opening/endgame knowledge, and custom VLSI hardware. It marked a public turning point: AI could beat elite human expertise in a highly symbolic game.

## People & Place
- Murray Campbell: Deep Blue team member

Key place: IBM Research

## Historical Background
Computer chess had advanced for decades through better search, evaluation, and hardware. Deep Blue made that lineage visible on a global stage.

## Canonical Source
Deep Blue journal article. https://doi.org/10.1016/S0004-3702(99)00044-5

## Core Idea
The machine searched many candidate moves deeply, pruning losing branches and scoring positions with chess knowledge.

## Key Concepts
- Alpha-Beta Search: A pruning method that avoids searching branches that cannot change the decision.
- Evaluation Function: Chess knowledge assigns a score to positions when search stops.
- Custom Hardware: Special chips accelerated chess move generation and evaluation.

## Impact
Deep Blue shaped public expectations for AI milestones and became a reference point for later game systems such as AlphaGo.
