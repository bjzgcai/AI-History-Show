# Pandemonium

## Full Name
Pandemonium

## Year / Period
1959

## Type
Perception

## One-Sentence Summary
Pandemonium is an AI100 achievement in machine perception.

## Hero Description
Pandemonium described perception as a layered competition among simple feature detectors and higher-level pattern recognizers. Its noisy, distributed voting metaphor anticipated later ideas in ensemble recognition, blackboard-style systems, and layered pattern processing.

## People & Place
- Oliver Selfridge: Author of the Pandemonium architecture

Key place: MIT Lincoln Laboratory

## Historical Background
Early AI needed models for pattern recognition that were less brittle than hand-coded one-shot templates.

## Canonical Source
Pandemonium: A Paradigm for Learning. https://doi.org/10.1007/978-1-4899-1221-6_4

## Core Idea
Multiple small recognizers respond to features; stronger responses propagate upward until a winning interpretation emerges.

## Key Concepts
- Feature Detectors: Small recognizers fire for local evidence.
- Layered Competition: Higher-level hypotheses compete using lower-level signals.
- Evidence Accumulation: Recognition emerges from many partial votes.

## Impact
The architecture became a classic reference for distributed recognition and for thinking about perception as evidence accumulation.
