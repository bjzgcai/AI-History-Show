## Full Name
The perceptron paper record

## Year / Period

## One-Sentence Summary
Rosenblatt Perceptron is an AI100 achievement in Neural networks.

## Hero Description
Rosenblatt's perceptron made neural learning tangible by pairing a simple threshold unit with an update rule. It promised machines that could learn from examples rather than being programmed only by explicit rules. The perceptron later became both a symbol of early neural optimism and a baseline for understanding linear classification.

## People & Place
- Frank Rosenblatt: Inventor of the perceptron

Key place: Cornell Aeronautical Laboratory

## Historical Background
In the 1950s, researchers were exploring machines inspired by neural and psychological models of perception.

## Canonical Source
The perceptron paper record. https://doi.org/10.1037/h0042519

## Core Idea
A perceptron computes a weighted sum of inputs and fires when the sum crosses a threshold; mistakes trigger weight updates.

## Key Concepts
- Threshold Unit: A neuron-like unit outputs one class when a weighted sum crosses a boundary.
- Weight Update: Errors change the weights so future predictions improve.
- Linear Separability: A single perceptron succeeds only when a straight boundary can separate classes.

## Impact
The perceptron shaped neural-network history, including later debates about linear separability and multilayer learning.
