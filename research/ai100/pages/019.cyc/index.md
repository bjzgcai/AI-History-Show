# Cyc

## Full Name
Cyc

## Year / Period
1984

## Type
Knowledge Representation

## One-Sentence Summary
Cyc is an AI100 achievement in common-sense knowledge representation.

## Hero Description
Cyc pursued an audacious symbolic-AI premise: common sense can be represented explicitly as a large ontology and rule base. Its knowledge engineers encoded concepts, relations, microtheories, and inference rules so software could reason beyond narrow expert-system domains.

## People & Place
- Douglas Lenat: Founder of the Cyc project
- R. V. Guha: Co-author of core Cyc representation work

Key place: Microelectronics and Computer Technology Corporation

## Historical Background
Expert systems worked in narrow domains but broke easily when ordinary background knowledge was missing.

## Canonical Source
Building Large Knowledge-Based Systems. https://dl.acm.org/doi/book/10.5555/70571

## Core Idea
Cyc organizes facts and rules into an ontology with contextual microtheories, then uses inference to answer questions across that structure.

## Key Concepts
- Ontology: Concepts and relations are encoded explicitly.
- Microtheory: Context-specific assumptions keep reasoning organized.
- Common Sense: Background knowledge is treated as a first-class AI resource.

## Impact
Cyc remains a touchstone for debates about symbolic AI, common sense, hybrid reasoning, and the limits of data-only learning.
