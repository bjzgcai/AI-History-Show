# SHRDLU

## Full Name
SHRDLU dissertation record

## Year / Period

## One-Sentence Summary
SHRDLU is an AI100 achievement in Natural language understanding.

## Hero Description
SHRDLU showed that a computer could carry on a constrained but surprisingly rich dialogue about a toy blocks world. Winograd combined parsing, planning, procedural semantics, and a small world model so natural-language commands could become robot-like actions. Its power came from tight integration: language worked because the world was limited and explicitly represented.

## People & Place
- Terry Winograd: Creator of SHRDLU

Key place: MIT Artificial Intelligence Laboratory

## Historical Background
Early language programs often handled isolated sentences. SHRDLU made language, reference, memory, and action share one miniature world.

## Canonical Source
SHRDLU dissertation record. https://dspace.mit.edu/handle/1721.1/7095

## Core Idea
The system understood utterances by grounding them in blocks, spatial relations, and procedures that could actually change the scene.

## Key Concepts
- Microworld: A narrow environment makes language and action tractable.
- Procedural Semantics: Meaning is tied to procedures the system can run.
- Reference Resolution: The system maps words like "the red block" to objects in the scene.

## Impact
SHRDLU became a classic example of both the promise and the brittleness of microworld AI.
